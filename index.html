Why Children Rights...?

Children and young people have the same general human rights as adults. Children are neither the property of their parents nor are they helpless objects of charity. They are human beings and are the subject of their own rights.
The Convention on the Rights of the Child sets out the rights that must be realized for children to develop to their full potential.

Our Role in thier Rights

We have a vital role in ensuring the well-being and dignity of children. They are the ones who can provide a safe and supportive environment for children to learn, grow, and express themselves. Children look up to their parents and elders for love, guidance, and inspiration. As John F. Kennedy said, "Children are the world's most valuable resource and its best hope for the future."
